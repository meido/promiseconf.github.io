Call for Papers: Journal Special Issue

RAISE:
Realizing AI Synergies
in Software Engineering

Aim

The aim of this SI is to promote the latest innovative synergistic AI and SE techniques and practices.

This topic has attracted much recent attention; at ICSE'13 we held a 2-day "Realizing AI Synergies in SE" (RAISE) workshop sponsored by the NSF. That workshop was very successful (30 participants) and generated much thought and debate; see:

  • Raise 2013: day 1
  • Raise 2013: day 2

About

Much of the innovation in the modern world is based on information technologies. Software engineering is now expected to solve a plethora of increasingly complex questions that are dynamic, automated, adaptive, or must execute on a very large scale.

In theory, AI technologies have the potential to support the development of increasingly complex SE systems. Conversely, in theory, SE might also play a role in alleviating development costs and the development effort associated with AI tools.

Topics of interest for this Special Issue on Realizing AI Synergies in Software Engineering include, but are not restricted to:

  • Agents & SE
  • Data Mining and SE
  • Bayesian belief networks
  • Spectra-based software diagnosis
  • Search-based software engineering
  • AI tools for system dynamics and simulation
  • Software metrics applied to AI techniques
  • AI techniques for validating software metrics
  • AI techniques for Testing and quality assurance
  • AI for Model-based SE and Model-driven engineering
  • Rapid prototyping and scripting for AI techniques
  • Software for knowledge acquisition and representation
  • Cognitive Psychology (and its implications for SE + AI)
  • AI techniques for Cost analysis and risk assessment in software projects
  • Knowledge representation, ontologies and reasoning and other semantic aspects in SE
  • AI techniques in Requirements engineering, specification, design and integration
  • AI techniques for Assessing the quality of datasets (imbalance, noise, missing values, etc.)
  • Machine Learning, Optimisation and Computational Intelligence techniques

Submission

Submission of a manuscript implies that the work described has not been published before;

  • i.e. that it is not under consideration for publication anywhere else; that its publication has been approved by all co-authors, if any, as well as by the responsible authorities - tacitly or explicitly - at the institute where the work has been carried out.
  • If an earlier version of this work has been published then this submission must be at least 30% different and the authors should explain in the introduction the delta between this new paper and the prior version

Formatting templates: http://www.springer.com/computer/ai/journal/10515?detailsPage=pltci_1060168 (Latex)

Papers should be submitted to the special issue through the Editorial Manager https://www.editorialmanager.com/ause/ , selecting the article type "Special Issue: Realizing Synergies in AI and SE".
